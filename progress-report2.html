Progress Update 2 (10/13/2021)

The next step was to figure out how exactly I would predict the stellar activity. Last year, I explored the scientific causes of various stellar phenomena, so I knew that solar flares are caused by the entangling of magnetic fields on the Sun. These magnetic fields can visibly be "seen" since they create sunspots on the Sun's surface. So, by analyzing sunspot intensity/proximity, I'll be able to determine the likelihood of a solar flare. The Solar Dynamics Observatory (SDO) and Space-Weather HMI Active Region Patch (SHARP) dataset provide day-by-data images of the Sun in various wavlengths, perfect for the purposes of this project. Since I decided to use image data, my best approach for analyzation would be using feature extraction. I researched a few different methods for feature extraction, and found a research paper and tutorial that are really helpful. Moving forward, I plan to replicate this feature extraction and understand scientifically what these features mean so that I may extract them from light curve data as well.
